About Me

My name is Adeel Younis, and I go by the pen name Aazaar. I am a student pursuing BBA and a passionate writer. I'm not a complete poet, but I love to rhyme verses in both English and Urdu. Writing and cricket are my two hobbies, and I prefer spending my time alone, deep in thought and creativity.

I reside in Faizpur, Sarai Alamgir, Gujrat, Punjab, Pakistan, and enjoy expressing my thoughts through poetry and letters to the world.

Silent Echoes

SILENT GOODBYE

Why must it be me,
The one to start, to set you free?
I wait, you don’t care,
This friendship feels unfair.
I’ve given all, you never see,
Now I stand alone, just me.
I could speak, but pride is high,
Why should I always be the guy?
You’ll see one day what I’ve become,
Living my life, feeling numb.
Without your words, without your call,
This friendship’s headed for a fall.
No more reaching, no more fight,
I’ll walk away, out of sight.
Let you wonder, let you see,
I can live without you—still be me.

UNANSWERED, UNNOTICED

Words unsaid, AAZAAR, but heavy still,
A silence no one can fill.
I ponder if you feel it too,
This quiet gap between us two.
I wonder why this happened so,
Was it your choice or life’s flow?
If you’re still there, just let me know,
A simple word, so I won’t feel low.
I miss the chats, the jokes, the fun,
Just say a word, even if it’s one.

Worried but Hopeful

I see my parents growing old,
For us, they remain strong and bold.
I wish I could ask them to rest,
But I have no job, no money to invest.
Each day I search, each day I try,
But opportunities seem to pass by.
Responsibilities heavy on my mind,
Future looks dark, no light I find.
Depression knocks, I feel the strain,
Sociophobia adds to the pain.
Yet deep inside, I still believe
What makes them proud, I can achieve.
But here I am, AAZAAR, with fears untold,
No one to hear, no hand I can hold.
I face it all, this lonely fight,
Finding a path, finding some light.

My Secrets

BETWEEN OBEDIENCE AND DESIRE

I'm stuck in a fate I didn't choose,
Duty calls, my heart feels loose.
Obedience is key, I need to obey,
No escape from it, night or day.
Torn between love and duty,
Lost in thoughts, searching for serenity.
In her eyes, a secret I'll find,
Keeping mine, a heart left behind.
Emotions raw, like an open sore,
In search of truth, for sure.
Accepting fate, I'll do my part,
Someday, I’ll enter love’s sweet mart.
Surrendering to duty, I'll play my role,
Afraid to dream, my heart's goal.
Bound by promises, I'll make it through,
Uncertain love, my heart feels blue.
Trying to move on, but memories stay,
In the shadows, a love will sway.
Whispering secrets, every single day,
In my dreams, a love will find its way.
Longing for freedom, beyond the chains,
Love's uncertainty, in shifting lanes.
Maybe I’ll be out of woes,
Accepting fate as it goes.
Resigned to duty, every day,
Remembering love, in a secret way.
Yearning for answers, AAZAAR, as time unfolds,
Hoping to find a way, as life molds.
Ending secrets, on the final day,
Revealing all, in a brand new way.

TEMPORARY BONDS

Friendship, a word so bright,
Fades like stars in sunlight.
Temporary ties, that's all I see,
Brief interactions, then goodbye to me.
I once expected more, a bond so strong,
But now I know, I’m alone all along.
No need for promises, or vows to make,
Just a hello, and a heart that won't ache.
If they reach out, I'll be there to share,
A smile, a laugh, and show I care.
No expectations, AAZAAR, just a friendly ear,
A hug, a hello, and a heart that's clear.
Few friendships meant to be, time pass,
Their termination resulted in no loss.
For change of sincerity, I waited,
Hurtful, they were just overrated.

An Unspoken Plea

What simply I want, why can’t she
Speak words of love to me?
If she dares to see what I will be,
Let her see, stay free.
Me being shy will simply deny,
Even saying her goodbye.
What actually makes me cry,
Is truth I know, people lie.
I just want her to say "HI."

Social & Political Opinions

Profit Over Education

"The population in Sarai Alamgir and Kharian primarily consists of middle and lower-middle-class individuals who cannot afford university education. The only option available is a single sub-campus of Punjab University in a neighboring city. As a result, private affiliated colleges are the preferred choice for most students, with a majority being girls and a smaller number of boys. Unfortunately, these colleges are not providing quality education and have several flaws that need to be addressed urgently.

The education standard in these colleges is subpar, with unqualified teachers and inadequate facilities. Unlike university education, there are no assignments, quizzes, or presentations, and teachers simply award grades without proper evaluation. The exam system is flawed, biased, and lacks proper invigilation, encouraging cheating. The affiliated universities fail to supervise exams effectively.

Moreover, these colleges charge exorbitant fees, blackmailing students by withholding roll number slips and result cards if payments are not made. This negatively impacts students' mental health and well-being, which is unethical and unacceptable. Furthermore, these colleges engage in paper leaking, solving, and awarding degrees without attendance, all for a few rupees. It is evident that these private colleges prioritize profit over students' futures.

Relevant authorities, including education ministries and universities, must address these issues to ensure students receive quality education. The future of our country depends on it."

Dated: July 3, 2024

Protest for Palestine

"There is no doubt that the situation in Gaza is extremely severe and alarming, necessitating urgent international attention and a peaceful resolution. The harrowing images of children and families affected by Israel's brutalities tell a devastating story, which has galvanized people worldwide to protest and seek justice. This motivation to protest intensifies when political leaders and legislators fail to act...

Protest is not always about achieving a specific goal but also about raising awareness and garnering support for a cause. While the government may need to maintain neutrality for diplomatic reasons, it cannot violate the Pakistani people's right to freedom of expression (Article 19) and freedom of assembly (Article 16) at any cost."

Dated: May 22, 2024

Traffic Woes in Sarai Alamgir

"Sarai Alamgir, situated in Gujrat district, faces serious traffic congestion issues, impacting daily life and emergency services. The town lies between the River Jhelum and the Upper Jhelum Canal, with the railway line and GT Road running parallel but perpendicular to the river and canal. This layout, coupled with over 60% of the population residing on the side away from the main GT road, necessitates crossing railway barriers to reach essential services like hospitals, schools, colleges, and government offices located on GT Road...

Urgent action, such as appointing traffic wardens and completing the bridge project, is needed to address these long-standing problems and improve the town's infrastructure and quality of life."

Dated: February 14, 2024

Embracing NOTA: Electoral Evolution

"I am writing to bring attention to the need for the inclusion of 'None of The Above' (NOTA) on the ballot paper in Pakistan's electoral system. This option allows voters to express their disapproval of candidates in the current voting system, a provision already adopted by many countries such as the US, France, and India...

The potential impact of NOTA cannot be overstated. It has the potential to address the trust deficit in the electoral process, tackle nepotism in ticket distribution, and alleviate voter turnout problems. The embarrassment of being rejected by NOTA may serve as a catalyst for improvement, urging politicians to perform better."

Dated: January 29, 2024
